Fact of the Day

Only two countries on Earth are ‘doubly landlocked’

Liechtenstein and Uzbekistan are the world’s sole double-landlocked nations — surrounded entirely by other landlocked countries, so reaching open sea from either means crossing at least two borders. Liechtenstein held that lonely distinction alone until Uzbekistan joined it in 1991, on breaking away from the Soviet Union.

You don’t need to be a coder or a maths genius to understand AI or start using it well. A little bit of regular practice — trying it out, reading a short course, asking questions — goes a long way. Here’s a simple way to get started, at your own pace.

Free Courses to Try

1

Elements of AI

A free, beginner-friendly course from the University of Helsinki. No maths or programming needed — takes about 6-8 hours and gives you a real certificate at the end.

2

AI For Everyone

Taught by AI expert Andrew Ng, free to watch and learn from (a small fee only applies if you want an official certificate). About 6 hours long.

3

Generative AI For Everyone

Explains tools like Claude and ChatGPT — what they can and can’t do, and how people are using them in everyday life and work.

Learn by Doing

1

Try Claude or ChatGPT on a small real task

Ask it to help plan a school project, explain a tricky homework topic a different way, or summarise a long article. Learning by using it beats reading about it.

2

Teachable Machine

A free, visual tool from Google that lets you train a simple AI model yourself using your webcam, drawings or sounds — no code required.

A Simple Way to Pace It

Week 1: Start Elements of AI, just the first couple of chapters.

Week 2: Try Claude or ChatGPT for a real school task, and finish another chapter or two.

Week 3: Play with Teachable Machine to see how an AI actually ‘learns’ from examples.

Week 4: Finish your course and try Generative AI For Everyone if you want to go a bit deeper.
