Getting Started with AI

You don’t need any technical background to get comfortable with AI — most of it is about curiosity, not maths. The courses below are free, friendly and self-paced, and the “learn by doing” tools let you try things for real. Twenty minutes here and there is plenty.

Free Courses to Try

1

Elements of AI

A free online introduction from the University of Helsinki — over 2 million students, no programming or complicated maths required. Verified live and free this run.

2

AI For Everyone (Coursera)

Andrew Ng’s classic non-technical course. Free to audit — you can watch all the videos without paying (certificates cost extra). Verified this run.

3

Generative AI for Everyone (Coursera)

The follow-up, all about tools like ChatGPT and Claude and how to use them at work. Also free to audit. Verified this run.

Learn by Doing

1

Claude

Try small real tasks: plan a trip, rewrite an email, explain a confusing letter. The fastest way to learn is to use it for things you actually need.

2

ChatGPT

Same idea, different assistant — comparing answers from two AIs is a great way to see their strengths and quirks.

3

Teachable Machine

Train your own tiny AI in the browser using your webcam — no code, weirdly fun, and it teaches you how “training” really works.

A Simple Way to Pace It

Week 1

Do the first chapter of Elements of AI and have one real conversation a day with Claude or ChatGPT.

Week 2

Start AI For Everyone (audit for free). Keep the daily chats going — try asking for step-by-step help with something fiddly.

Week 3

Play with Teachable Machine for an afternoon, then finish AI For Everyone.

Week 4

Start Generative AI for Everyone and pick one weekly task (meal planning, emails, revision help) to hand to an AI from now on.
