Getting Started with AI

You don't need to code or have a technical background to understand what AI actually is and how it's used. These are absolute-beginner-friendly places to start — no experience required.

Free Courses to Try

1

Elements of AI

A free, widely-recommended introduction from the University of Helsinki and MinnaLearn — a few hours to go from "I don't understand any of this" to a solid grasp of the basics. No coding involved.

elementsofai.com · free, no coding required
2

AI For Everyone (DeepLearning.AI, Andrew Ng)

A non-technical overview of what AI can and can't do, from one of the field's best-known teachers. Free to audit on Coursera — look for the "audit" option during sign-up rather than the paid certificate track.

coursera.org · free to audit
3

Generative AI For Everyone

The follow-up course focused specifically on tools like ChatGPT and Claude — what generative AI is, where it's useful, and its limitations. Also free to audit.

coursera.org · free to audit

Learn by Doing

1

Just start talking to one

The fastest way to understand AI is to use it. Open Claude or ChatGPT (both free to try) and ask it to explain something you're curious about, draft an email, or plan a recipe — then ask it to explain its own reasoning.

claude.ai · chatgpt.com · free tiers available
2

Teachable Machine

A free, playful Google tool that lets you train a simple AI model with your webcam in minutes — a hands-on way to see how machine learning actually works under the hood.

teachablemachine.withgoogle.com · free, no login needed

A Simple Way to Pace It

Week 1: work through Elements of AI's first few chapters — just the concepts, no pressure. Week 2: open Claude or ChatGPT daily for small real tasks (emails, summaries, planning) to build a feel for what it's good at. Week 3: try Teachable Machine for a concrete, visual sense of how models learn. Week 4: revisit anything confusing from Week 1 now that you've used the tools — it usually clicks the second time round.
