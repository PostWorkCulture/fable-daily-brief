A Chinese AI company just built something that beats the big US ones

Getting into AI doesn’t need any coding or technical background — just curiosity. Here’s a simple way to start learning what AI actually is and how to use it, at your own pace.

Free Courses to Try

1

Elements of AI

A free course from the University of Helsinki that explains what AI is and how it works, with no maths or coding needed. Over 2 million people worldwide have taken it, and the certificate is free too.

2

AI For Everyone

Taught by AI expert Andrew Ng, this short course (under 10 hours) explains AI basics for complete beginners. You can watch all the videos for free; there’s only a cost if you want the certificate.

3

Generative AI For Everyone

Also by Andrew Ng, this one focuses on the AI tools that create text, images and more — how they work and how people are using them.

Learn by Doing

1

Try Claude or ChatGPT on a small real task

Ask it to help plan a school project, explain a tricky homework topic step by step, or summarise an article — using AI on something real is the fastest way to understand what it’s actually good at.

2

Teachable Machine

A free Google tool that lets you train your own simple AI model in your browser — for example, teaching it to recognise different objects or sounds — no coding required.

A Simple Way to Pace It

Week 1: Start Elements of AI — just the first couple of chapters, no pressure to rush.

Week 2: Keep going with Elements of AI, and try Teachable Machine for half an hour.

Week 3: Watch or work through AI For Everyone, and use Claude or ChatGPT for one real homework task.

Week 4: Try Generative AI For Everyone, and finish off whichever course you started — grab the free certificate if you want one.
