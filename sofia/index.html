This Week's Product Signal

OpenAI and Anthropic escalated the "agentic workplace" race this week. OpenAI launched ChatGPT Work on 9 July — folding its Codex coding agent into ChatGPT and powering it with the new GPT-5.6 model — as a direct answer to Anthropic's Claude Cowork, which expanded to mobile and web on 7 July. Meanwhile SpaceXAI's Grok 4.5 launched publicly on 9 July and ranked fourth on Artificial Analysis's Intelligence Index (score 54, behind Claude Fable 5, GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.8) at a fraction of rivals' cost, and Google is reportedly delaying Gemini 3.5 Pro to 17 July for a full architecture rebuild — all useful build-vs-buy data points if you're benchmarking AI features this quarter.

The PM role is visibly bifurcating, and it shows up in pay. Industry research this month puts AI-native "builder" PMs (who ship prototypes themselves and blur the PM/engineer line) at roughly $245,000 average US compensation, versus $123,000 for traditional generalist PMs — alongside a second "integrator" track for high-EQ PMs who own the roadmap in messy, cross-functional B2B environments. Worth mapping your own scope against both tracks.

LSEG deepens its AI-data infrastructure push. LSEG (London Stock Exchange Group) has partnered with Databricks to make LSEG financial data natively available for building AI-driven agents and analytics apps, and launched a "Model-as-a-Service" capability letting institutions host and distribute analytical models through a centralised marketplace — Societe Generale is an early provider. Separately, Q1 2026 M&A activity hit a five-year high (+27% YoY), fuelling demand for the deal-data and workflow tools that companies like 9fin and S&P Global build.
