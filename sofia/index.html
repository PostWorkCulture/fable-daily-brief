Getting Started with AI

You don’t need to code or be a maths genius to understand AI — the best AI courses for beginners use plain language and everyday examples. A few hours a week is plenty, and everything below is free. Start anywhere; curiosity is the only requirement.

Free Courses to Try

1

Elements of AI

University of Helsinki’s famous free course — no maths or programming needed, about 6–8 hours total, with a free certificate at the end. Over 2 million people have taken it.

Free · verified live 22 Jul
2

AI For Everyone

Andrew Ng’s classic non-technical introduction on Coursera — free to audit (you only pay if you want the certificate).

Free to audit · verified live 22 Jul
3

Generative AI for Everyone

Also by Andrew Ng — explains how tools like Claude and ChatGPT actually work and how to prompt them well. No prior knowledge needed.

Free to audit · verified live 22 Jul

Learn by Doing

1

Try small real tasks with Claude

Ask it to plan a revision timetable, explain a tricky homework topic, or brainstorm story ideas — you learn fastest by using it on things you actually care about.

claude.ai
2

Compare with ChatGPT

Try the same task in ChatGPT and notice how the answers differ — comparing tools teaches you what AI is good and bad at.

chatgpt.com
3

Train your own model with Teachable Machine

Google’s free tool lets you train a simple image or sound recogniser in your browser in minutes — no code, weirdly fun.

teachablemachine.withgoogle.com

A Simple Way to Pace It

Week 1: Start Elements of AI — just the first two chapters.

Week 2: Finish Elements of AI and try three real tasks in Claude.

Week 3: Watch AI For Everyone’s first two weeks of videos; build something in Teachable Machine.

Week 4: Try Generative AI for Everyone and write down three ways you’d use AI at school or in a hobby.
