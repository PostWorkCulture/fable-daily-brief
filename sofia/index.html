This Week's Product Signal

Two frontier launches landed the same day — July 9. OpenAI made its GPT-5.6 family (Sol, Terra and Luna) publicly available across ChatGPT and the API, with Terra pitched squarely at "high-volume business tasks like customer support, internal tools and document analysis" — exactly the workflows B2B data platforms are racing to automate. A day earlier, xAI launched Grok 4.5, trained on real Cursor coding sessions and priced over 60% below Claude Opus 4.8 or GPT-5.5, sharpening the price war for any product embedding a frontier model. For anyone benchmarking build-vs-buy on AI features this quarter, both are worth a look.

LSEG deepens its AI-data infrastructure push. LSEG (London Stock Exchange Group) has partnered with Databricks to make LSEG financial data natively available for building AI-driven agents and analytics apps, and launched a "Model-as-a-Service" capability letting institutions host and distribute analytical models through a centralised marketplace — Societe Generale is an early provider. Separately, Q1 2026 M&A activity hit a five-year high (+27% YoY), fuelling demand for the deal-data and workflow tools that companies like 9fin and S&P Global build.

Similarweb is mid-CEO-succession, and the "PM role is splitting" debate is heating up. Similarweb's board announced a formal CEO search as founder Or Offer plans to step down by mid-2027. Industry commentary this quarter is converging on a theme: the PM role is bifurcating into deeply technical "AI-native" builders versus growth/monetisation specialists — worth watching if you're benchmarking your own scope.
