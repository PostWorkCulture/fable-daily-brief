Build & Apply — This Month's Plan

Learn (free, credible)

Read the UK Government AI Playbook — it's the shared language of every public-sector AI interview. Follow it with the LGA AI case-study bank (learn 5 real council deployments cold, including Kingston's) and the MHCLG Local AI blog. That trio makes you conversant with how government actually buys and governs AI.

Develop (evidence beats certificates)

Build a small portfolio: one page each on three council problems AI could solve, with the use case, risks, human-in-the-loop design and a measurable outcome. This is exactly the artefact adoption roles produce, and almost no candidate brings one.

Find (where the jobs live)

Set alerts on Civil Service Jobs ("artificial intelligence", filter to policy/delivery, not engineering), Jobsgopublic and LGA jobs for councils, and watch Kingston and Elmbridge career pages directly — a council already piloting AI will need adoption support next. The Turing Institute and techUK events double as hiring pipelines.

This week's move

Three deadlines land within a week of each other: both GovAI fellowship tracks close this Sunday, the Cabinet Office director role closes Monday, and Defra's Head of Responsible AI role follows on 15 July. Prioritise whichever fits your evidence base best and get an application in — even a near-miss is a credible line for the CV.
